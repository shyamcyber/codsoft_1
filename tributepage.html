Tribute to Mother Teresa
1910 - 1997

Mary Teresa Bojaxhiu MC (26 August 1910 - 5 September 1997), better known as Mother Teresa, She was an Albanian Catholic nun and the founder of the Missionaries of Charity.

Born in Skopje, then part of the Ottoman Empire, at the age of 18 she moved to Ireland and later to India, where she lived most of her life. On 4 September 2016.

She was canonised by the Catholic Church as Saint Teresa of Calcutta. The anniversary of her death, 5 September, is her feast day.

Mother Teresa will always be remembered for their dedication, passion, and remarkable achievements. Their legacy continues to inspire and influence future generations.
